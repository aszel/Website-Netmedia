EMIKA

Development of multimedia, dynamic museum information systems with integrated functions to the interaction with digital art objects, individual exhibit catalogue generation and inclusion of service offers (EMIKA)

At the project EMIKA the FHTW Berlin co-operates with the endowment Jewish museum of Berlin. In the main focus are the interdisciplinary research and the development of museum information systems as well as the qualification of information scientists and mind scientists.

In the project employees of different disciplines like cultural scientist, historian, museum manager, creator, information scientist, communication scientist and economist are represented. In the group between FHTW Berlin, the endowment Jewish museum of Berlin as well as small and middle-class enterprises of the region Berlin-Brandenburg applications and services are developed since October 2006 exemplary for the following areas and are applied specifically practically:

  • The museum information systems from which information can be spent automatically and partly specifically for visitor in different forms and for different devices (eLearning and centre of information, personalised catalogues, Internet, mobile devices)
  • Mobile, digital leaders for museums and exhibits on the information for the visitors
  • Production of personalised exhibit catalogues (printed or digitally) considering the copyright
  • Interactive action fields to the communicative inclusion and intensified employment of the exhibits by the visitors of museums, galleries and exhibits
  • eBar to the extension of museums and exhibits after the real rooms and buildings.

With end of the project it should be checked whether the foundation of a technology-oriented service enterprise is possible. This would occur together with the founders of a new business centre in the FHTW Berlin. In addition, deficits should be diminished with specific continuing education arrangements and new competence with the project participants should been set up.
